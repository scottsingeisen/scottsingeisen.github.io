Projects

  • 2024.01 - Present
    NCCU Eyrie Initiative Community Design Center
    First community design center at an HBCU in North Carolina, directing community-engaged design research and practice. Current work includes the Fayetteville Street / Hayti Heritage Corridor Initiative (Preservation Durham, $10,000 contract) and 10 National Trust HOPE Crew student fellowships ($50,000 total).
    • LS3P Foundation: $142,500 (2025–2028)
    • Preservation Durham contract: $10,000 (2026)
  • 2025.01 - Present
    CLOUD Collaborative Research Project
    Practice-based research initiative treating atmospheric clouds as a vehicle for exploring computational form, representation, and metaphor, using Python and Blender. Co-authorship of the 2025 cataloging framework and public contributor call.
  • 2024.01 - Present
    Digital Eagles Nest
    Cross-disciplinary digital humanities project capturing and digitizing NCCU's historic campus through 3D scanning, photogrammetry, archival research, and virtual presentation — blending architectural, cultural, and social histories into a public digital campus resource.
  • 2024.01 - Present
    Fine Art Printmaking & Architectural Image Production
    Research examining how lithography and fine art printmaking shaped the public reception of major American architectural projects — an underexplored intersection of architectural history and art history. NEH Award for Faculty at HBCUs submitted ($37,500, under review).
