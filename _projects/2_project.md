The Digital Eagles Nest is a cross-disciplinary digital humanities initiative capturing and digitizing the historic campus of North Carolina Central University through 3D scanning, photogrammetry, archival research, and virtual presentation.

The project blends architectural, cultural, and social histories into a public digital campus resource — modeled on similar efforts such as [Building South Bend](https://buildingsouthbend.nd.edu) and [Paris: Past and Present](http://paris.cdh.ucla.edu). NCCU's campus is rich in architectural history, with eleven structures predating 1940 contributing to its heritage, and the campus listed on the National Register of Historic Places.

The Digital Eagles Nest was developed through a NCCU-Duke University Digital Humanities Course Development Fellowship, supported by the Mellon Humanities Unbounded initiative at Duke University. Each offering of the course expands the archive as students capture additional buildings and layer in social, cultural, and institutional histories.
