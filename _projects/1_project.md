The Eyrie Initiative Community Design Center is the first community design center established at an HBCU in North Carolina. Founded in 2024 at North Carolina Central University, the Eyrie Initiative advances community-engaged design research and practice in Durham and the surrounding region.

Current work includes a multi-partner corridor study along Fayetteville Street and the Hayti Heritage District in partnership with Preservation Durham, and the coordination of ten National Trust HOPE Crew student fellowships providing hands-on preservation experience to NCCU students.

The Eyrie Initiative is supported by a $142,500 grant from the LS3P Foundation (2025–2028) and a $10,000 contract with Preservation Durham (2026).
