Prof. Singeisen presented "Architecture Makes Strange Bedfellows: The Workmanship of Risk and Certainty in Digital Design Education" at the 40th Annual Conference of the National Conference on the Beginning Design Student (NCBDS 40), hosted by the North Carolina State University College of Design in Raleigh, NC.

---

#### About the Paper

The paper examines the tension between risk and certainty in digital design pedagogy — drawing on David Pye's distinction between the workmanship of risk and the workmanship of certainty to interrogate what is gained and lost as computational tools reduce the unpredictability historically inherent in design making. The work contributes to Singeisen's ongoing research in beginning design pedagogy and the role of digital tools in shaping architectural thinking.

NCBDS is the premier national forum for scholarship in beginning design education, bringing together faculty from architecture, landscape architecture, and related design disciplines across the country.
