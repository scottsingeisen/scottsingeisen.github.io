Prof. Scott Singeisen and North Carolina Central University announce a $142,500 grant from the LS3P Foundation to initiate the NCCU Eyrie Initiative, a project designed to advance inclusive excellence, well-being, and social justice in architecture. NCCU is one of only two recipients nationwide, recognized alongside the University of Florida.

---

#### The Eyrie Initiative Community Design Center

At the heart of the initiative is the newly established Community Design Center, aimed at addressing social equity and health challenges in Durham and the Southeast. The center engages students in direct collaborations with local organizations, fostering a hands-on approach to creating sustainable, inclusive, and equitable design solutions — connecting the university with its community.

Prof. Singeisen stated: "The NCCU Eyrie Initiative repositions our architectural studies as a center of innovative learning and profound social impact. This project empowers our students to actively participate in and lead the creation of equitable design solutions that reflect diverse community needs." The initiative also prioritizes diversity in the profession, inviting BIPOC, Black, and women architects as mentors and guest lecturers to enrich students' learning experiences and career opportunities.

#### About the LS3P Foundation

The LS3P Foundation, a nonprofit initiative committed to enhancing southeastern communities through the built environment, was founded in 2022 by LS3P. The Foundation supports leading design thinkers in advancing research, strategies, and technologies that promote transformative change, improve human and planetary well-being, increase economic mobility, and advance issues of health and social equity across the Southeast.
