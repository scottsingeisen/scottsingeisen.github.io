The Eyrie Initiative Community Design Center has partnered with the National Trust for Historic Preservation's HOPE Crew program to bring ten paid summer fellowships to NCCU students — $50,000 in direct student support for hands-on preservation work along the Fayetteville Street Corridor.

---

#### HOPE Crew at NCCU

HOPE Crew is a nationally recognized initiative that trains young people in historic preservation through paid, project-based fieldwork. Student participants work alongside seasoned craft experts on real preservation projects, gaining professional skills and direct field experience.

The Eyrie Initiative plays a coordination and community engagement role in the partnership, connecting the work to the broader goals of the department and the Fayetteville Street / Hayti Heritage Corridor initiative. The National Trust manages all project logistics, liability, and property approvals directly.

On April 14, an information session drew seventeen students from across the Department of Art & Design. The session was led by Milan Jordan, Senior Director of Preservation Programs at the National Trust, and Molly Baker, HOPE Crew Manager, who traveled from Washington, DC and New Orleans, respectively, to present the program in person.

The fellowship expanded from an initial six positions to ten, bringing total student support to $50,000 for the summer — a meaningful financial opportunity alongside a distinctive professional experience for students in pre-architecture, graphic design, and history.
