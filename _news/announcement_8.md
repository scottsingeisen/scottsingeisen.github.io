The Eyrie Initiative Community Design Center has been awarded a $10,000 design contract with Preservation Durham for a corridor study and urban design initiative along Fayetteville Street and the Hayti Heritage District — one of Durham's most historically significant corridors.

---

#### The Fayetteville Street / Hayti Heritage Corridor

The Hayti neighborhood was among the most prosperous Black communities in the American South before urban renewal in the 1960s severed it from downtown Durham. Fayetteville Street, which connects NCCU directly to the heart of that history, is now the subject of a multi-partner planning and design effort to which the Eyrie Initiative brings an HBCU-based community design perspective.

The contract represents the Eyrie Initiative's first formal practice engagement and positions NCCU students in direct contact with the built environment questions shaping their own city. Preservation Durham is one of North Carolina's leading historic preservation organizations, with deep roots in the documentation and advocacy of Durham's African American architectural heritage.
